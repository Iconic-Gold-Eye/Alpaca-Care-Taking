Internal Parasites

Internal Parasites are organisms like worms that penetrate an alpaca.

Causes

Internal Parasites are caused by a broad range of organisms, but the most common parasite is Eimeria punoensis. From 2010 to 2011, alpacas in Japan were tested by the NIH for gastrointestinal parasites. 69.8% of the alpacas were infected with Eimeria punoensis.

Common Targets

Internal parasites often affect the following

  • Young Alpacas
  • Immunocompromised Alpacas

Symptoms

See your veteranarian for treatment if your camelid has the following symptoms

  • Weight Loss
  • Amenia

Treatment

External Parasites can be treated with anthelmintics or Coccidiostats.
