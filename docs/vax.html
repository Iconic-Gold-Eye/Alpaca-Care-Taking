Vaccination

  • Alpaca vaccines are 5-in-1 vaccines (CDT) used for sheep and goats that protect against clostridial diseases. Clostridial diseases include tetanus, black disease, blackleg, pulpy kidney, and malignant oedema which can make the alpaca die suddenly
  • Crias get a vaccine at 6-8 weeks old, while adults get 2 at minimum per year. Pregnant alpacas get double dosage
  • Alpacas may or may not need to get a rabies and west nile virus vaccination
  • Needles should go in the fat layer (Under the skin but not into the muscle layer)
